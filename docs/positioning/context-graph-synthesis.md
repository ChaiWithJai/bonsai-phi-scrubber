## Part 1: The Central Theory

### Win = Mass x Energy x Coordination

Jiang Xueqin's Universal Law of Game Theory states that whoever wins any game
maximizes **Mass x Energy x Coordination** — where coordination is 4x more
important than mass, and energy is 2x more important than mass.

This is not an analogy. It is the central theory. Newton, Einstein, and Dante
each proved it at a different scale — and every worked example since is a
fractal derivative of the same pattern.

### The Three Proofs

**Newton (Mass — the laws that prove the lawgiver):**
Newton's three laws are not merely physics. They are proofs of God — the
demonstration that the universe follows mathematical structure, which implies a
designer. The first law (an object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon)
proves that something external must initiate motion. The second (F=ma) proves
that change is proportional and designed. The third (every action has an equal
and opposite reaction) proves that the universe is balanced. Mass is not inert
matter. Mass is the substrate that follows laws — and the laws themselves are
the proof of the designer.

**Einstein (Energy — a different model of reality):**
E=mc² is not "energy transforms mass." It is a **reordering** — a fundamentally
different ontological claim. Mass and energy are equivalent. They are the same
thing, observed from different frames. This is not a conversion formula. It is
the revelation that what we thought were two separate categories (matter and
energy) are one reality experienced differently. Einstein doesn't extend Newton.
He offers a different model of reality — one where the substrate itself is
fluid, convertible, and far more potent than Newton's static laws would suggest.

**Dante (Coordination — the soul is where you place your love):**
Dante transcends both Newton and Einstein by introducing the element that
neither physics can account for: **choice**. "The soul is where you place your
love." In the *Commedia*, Dante doesn't just observe the structure of reality
(Newton) or discover its hidden equivalences (Einstein). He **acts**. He decides
where to place his attention, his love, his will. The Inferno is the
consequence of disordered love. The Purgatorio is the discipline of reordering
love. The Paradiso is the fulfillment of love properly placed — converging on
the *punto*, the single point from which all meaning radiates.

Coordination in the equation is not teamwork. It is the **divine spark** — the
capacity to decide, to choose where to place your love, and thereby to create
reality. Dante's *Commedia* is not the "coordination component" of the
equation. It is a **worked example** of the entire equation — mass (the
universe's structure), energy (the will to move through it), coordination (the
choice of where to place love).

## Part 4: The PHI Scrubber as Instantiation

### Where we are on the UVI scale

The UVI (Usability, Value, Impact) scale positions work by what it
actually does for the user:

| Level | Definition | PHI Scrubber position |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Usability** | The tool works. Users can operate it. | Yes — the demo runs, the eval passes, the Loom video shows the flow. |
| **Value** | The tool does useful work in a real workflow. It pipes data, transforms it, moves it. | **We are here.** The scrubber helps with data piping: raw note → scrubbed record → clean Slack egress. It is not the final job (not therapy, not compliance, not the care record system). |
| **Impact** | The tool changes outcomes. Fewer privacy breaches, better care coordination, new workflows that weren't possible before. | Not yet. This requires clinical validation, legal framework, and real (or clinically-validated synthetic) workload testing. |

**Why Value, not Impact:** The PHI Scrubber helps with data piping but not the
final job. It scrubs notes and pipes clean records to Slack. It does not:
- Guarantee HIPAA compliance (organizational, not per-tool)
- Replace clinical judgment about what should be redacted
- Provide the care itself
- Prove zero re-identification risk

Saying we're at Value is honest. It's also the right position for recruiting
clinical experts — they see a tool that does real work but needs their expertise
to reach Impact.

### What the eval plan measures (prior art)

The eval plan references two bodies of prior art:

**1. Shreya Shankar / Hamel Husain (evals framework):**
- Error Analysis → Measure → Improve lifecycle
- Interactive human-in-the-loop review (not automated end-to-end)
- Failure mode taxonomy as a living, versioned artifact
- Breadth-first (cover all failure modes) then depth (many examples of each)
- Iterate over data — new failure modes emerge on re-review

Applied to PHI Scrubber:
- **Error analysis** is taste-specific → clinical experts must do it
- **Measurement** can be automated → Devin runbooks
- **Improvement** is iterative → prompt/recognizer changes, regression testing
- **Interactive review** → trace review interface (HTML report showing original
  vs scrubbed, highlighting catches/misses/over-redactions)

**2. PrismML whitepapers (model thesis):**
- Intelligence density: −log(error rate) / model size
- Native 1-bit training (not post-training quantization)
- The model is a port in the PHI Scrubber — we don't extend the whitepaper
  benchmarks because the whitepaper measures *model quality* and we measure
  *application reliability in a workflow*

These are different things:

| Whitepaper benchmarks | PHI Scrubber eval |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| MMLU Redux, GSM8K, ARC-C | Recall, precision, leakage, hard-case recall |
| Model quality across tasks | Application reliability in one workflow |
| Intelligence density (ratio metric) | Zero leakage (binary gate) |
| Compared to Qwen3, Llama3, Mistral | Compared to rules-only baseline |

**The focused thing:** Don't extend the whitepaper benchmarks. The eval plan
measures whether the scrubber reliably catches PHI in synthetic coaching notes.
That's it. The whitepaper is cited as the model thesis; Shreya/Hamel are cited
as the eval methodology. The PHI Scrubber eval is its own thing.

## Part 5: The Plan for Clinical Experts

### What makes this worthy of clinical attention

The pitch to clinical experts is not "we built an AI tool." It's:

1. **We have a working reference** — not a slide deck, a running system with
   100% recall on 21 synthetic notes and a verifier gate that blocks egress.
2. **We have an eval framework** — Shreya/Hamel's lifecycle, adapted for PHI
   scrubbing, with concrete runbooks.
3. **We have a contribution path** — clinical experts can submit synthetic
   notes, review traces, build the failure mode taxonomy, and see the effect
   on metrics. Their judgment shapes the system.
4. **We have guardrails** — we say "scrubbed," not "de-identified." We say
   "synthetic only." We name the precision tradeoff and the open gaps.

### The MMO case for clinical experts

| | Clinical Expert View | Jai's MMO View |
|---|---|---|
| **Means** | "I can review synthetic traces in a structured interface and see my feedback change the system's behavior." | The eval infrastructure exists. The contribution path is defined. The expert's time converts directly into a better failure mode taxonomy. |
| **Motivation** | "I care about patient privacy. I see AI tools being deployed without clinical oversight. This project invites me in early, with honest limits." | Healthcare privacy is the motivating vertical. The tool is positioned at Value (data piping), not Impact (compliance). That honesty is what earns trust. |
| **Opportunity** | "Edge AI is new. If I help shape the eval framework now, my domain expertise becomes part of the reference architecture that other builders copy." | The window is open. PrismML just launched. The eval framework is being built. The first clinical expert to contribute shapes everything that follows. |

### The Devin angle

Devin runbooks make the clinical expert's contribution scalable:

1. Expert submits a synthetic note + expected redactions
2. Devin runs the eval automatically (cloud-OK: synthetic data only)
3. Devin generates a trace review report (HTML)
4. Expert reviews, annotates failure modes
5. Devin updates the failure mode taxonomy and re-runs metrics
6. Expert sees the effect of their contribution

This is the Shreya pattern — human judgment drives error analysis, AI/automation
scales measurement and regression testing.
